Graphical Integrity: Lie Factor
• Lie Factor = (Size of effect in graphic)/(Size of effect in data)
• Lie Factor = >1, overstating
• Lie Factor = 1, accurate
• Lie Factor = <1, understating
Tufte, “Visual Display of Quantitative Information” (1983)

ChartJunk
• All elements in visualization that are not necessary for interpreting the information 
related to the data being shown
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chartjunk
• Heavy or dark grid lines
• Unnecessary text
• Inappropriately complex 
or gimmicky font faces
• Ornamented chart axes
• Backgrounds or icons 
within data graphs
• Ornamental shading and 
unnecessary dimensions

Human Limitations for Visualization
• The Magic Number Seven (7 ± 2) for visualization
• Not more than 7 ± 2 segments in a pie chart 
• Not more than 7 ± 2 colors in a line chart 
• and so on …..
Miller, G.. (1956). "The magical number seven, plus or minus two: Some limits on our capacity for processing information".

Colormaps
• Not all colormaps are equally effective
• Traditionally (default) used “Rainbow” colormap is actually one of the 
worst colormaps you can use!
• Perceptual Non-Uniformity: Inconsistent rate of change of color
• Artificial Boundaries: Change between green to yellow
• Rainbow colormaps are not colorblind-friendly
